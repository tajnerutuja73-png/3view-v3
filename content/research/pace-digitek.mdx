India's most advanced integrated BESS manufacturer-EPC-BOO platform is transitioning from a telecom infrastructure executor into a clean energy company, with an Rs. 11,338 Cr order book (4.3x FY26 revenue), 10 GWh of manufacturing capacity by October 2026 (the largest in India), and management revenue guidance of Rs. 3,200–3,400 Cr for FY27E (+21–29% YoY). ## 1. Investment Summary

**Business model.** Pace Digitek operates across two verticals: (a) Telecom & ICT — towers, OFC, Railways, O&M (54.6% of FY26 revenue); and (b) Energy — BESS manufacturing, EPC, and BOO (45.4%). Through subsidiary Lineage Power, Pace manufactures cell-to-pack-to-container BESS systems at Bidadi, Karnataka. It runs a three-mode energy monetisation model: Supply (12–14% EBITDA margin), EPC (7–10%), and BOO — Build-Own-Operate at 13–14% equity IRR.

**Why the thesis holds together.** (1) An Rs. 11,338 Cr executable order book — 4.3x revenue cover — with PSU counterparties (MSEDCL, KREDL, NTPC, SECI, BSNL). (2) Manufacturing capacity scaling 4x from 2.5 to 10 GWh by October 2026 — no other Indian company is at this scale. (3) Finance cost roughly halved post-IPO (Rs. 115 Cr → Rs. 60 Cr); credit rating upgraded from BBB- to A-. (4) L&T switched from Chinese BESS suppliers to Pace (a 250 MWh order in January 2026) — a notable competitive validation.

**Order book by segment (May-26):** Energy BOO Rs. 4,433 Cr (39.1%); Energy EPC Rs. 4,421 Cr (39.0%); Telecom EPC + O&M Rs. 2,040 Cr (18.0%); Telecom Supply + Others Rs. 444 Cr (3.9%). Key energy clients include KREDL (Rs. 1,775 Cr), MESDCL (Rs. 1,258 Cr), SECI-EPC (Rs. 1,159 Cr), MAHAGENCO (Rs. 920 Cr), SECI-BOO (Rs. 700 Cr), KPTCL (Rs. 700 Cr) and NTPC (Rs. 584 Cr); the telecom anchor is BSNL (Rs. 1,500 Cr).

> **Analyst takeaway:** The market currently prices Pace as a telecom EPC company (roughly 12x P/E). The business is becoming an energy manufacturer, a category where peers trade at 20–35x. The gap between those two multiples is the core source of the thesis's upside.

## 3. Company Overview

**History and pivot.** Incorporated in 2007 as Pace Power Systems, the company entered telecom infrastructure in 2011 and acquired Lineage Power (formerly GE Power Electronics India) in 2014, anchoring its manufacturing platform. It won the landmark BSNL 4G Saturation Project (Rs. 7,568 Cr, 9,595 sites) in FY23, listed on the BSE/NSE in FY26 raising Rs. 819 Cr, and commissioned its BESS manufacturing facility at Bidadi, Karnataka in FY26, delivering 178 BESS containers.

**Revenue mix evolution.** Energy was roughly 5.6% of FY25 revenue. By FY26 it had grown to 45.4% of revenue (Rs. 1,198 Cr) and 78.1% of the order book — a structural shift inside 24 months. Telecom & ICT remains 54.6% of FY26 revenue (Rs. 1,443 Cr) but is becoming the steady base while energy drives growth.

**Manufacturing.** Three Karnataka facilities: Kumbalgodu (telecom infra equipment, 10,944 unit capacity), Kumbalgodu (Li-ion batteries & PCS, 21,590 units), and Bidadi (BESS containers, 2.5 GWh scaling to 10 GWh by October 2026). In-house container fabrication is being added in July 2026 for cost and logistics control.

**Competitive positioning.** Pace is among a small number of Indian companies with full BESS vertical integration — cell-to-pack-to-container, integrated PCS & EMS, grid-scale EPC, BOO asset ownership, and cloud NOC monitoring. Per the chairman's concall remarks, no other company has manufactured 178 containers within India to date. L&T's switch from Chinese BESS suppliers to Pace, after completing its evaluation process, is cited as the strongest third-party validation available (a 250 MWh order in January 2026).

**Customers and geography.** Government-dominated customer base: BSNL (telecom); MESDCL, KREDL, KPTCL, SECI, NTPC, NLC India, DVC (energy). Private customers include L&T (BESS), Tata Teleservices (O&M) and Reliance Jio (supply). Top-3 customers were approximately 89% of FY25 revenue, a concentration management says is reducing as energy adds new PSU clients; average customer tenure among top customers is 4–6 years. Geographic exposure is over 95% India, with international operations in South Africa and Myanmar (telecom) and expansion planned into Africa (via an NEC XON partnership from FY27), Saudi Arabia and Kenya.

## 4. Industry Overview

**BESS — structural demand.** India's BESS capacity additions are projected to grow from roughly 7 GW (FY27E) to roughly 47 GW (FY32E) per the National Energy Policy 2023. Battery storage costs have fallen roughly 90% over the past decade, making grid-scale BESS increasingly viable. The Ministry of Power has tendered large BESS capacity through SECI, MSEDCL, NTPC, NLC and various state discoms, and a Viability Gap Funding rate of roughly Rs. 27 lakh/MWh supports project economics for BOO developers.

**Policy tailwinds.** The National Electricity Policy 2026 and Make-in-India initiatives favour domestic BESS manufacturers; an ALMM framework for BESS is expected to create a "buy domestic" mandate. State-level mandates in Maharashtra, Gujarat and Rajasthan require BESS alongside solar installations of 50 MW or more, opening a commercial-and-industrial market where Pace expects certifications ready by Q1 FY27.

**Telecom infrastructure.** India's telecom tower count is projected by CRISIL to grow from roughly 805,000 (FY24) to 1.0–1.035 million by FY28, driven by 5G densification, BSNL's 4G network expansion, the Railway Kavach safety system, and OFC rollout under BharatNet. The passive telecom infrastructure market is estimated at Rs. 2,000–2,100 billion cumulatively through FY28.

**Why Pace specifically benefits.** Unlike pure EPC players, Pace's integrated manufacturing lets it bid competitively while maintaining margins, accelerates execution through an in-house supply chain, captures value across the full BESS lifecycle (manufacturing → EPC → BOO → O&M), and leverages 18-plus years of government relationships built in telecom to win BESS orders — a distribution advantage management argues new entrants cannot replicate quickly.

## 5. Investment Thesis — Five Pillars

**1. Order book visibility — 4.3x revenue cover.** An Rs. 11,338 Cr executable order book against Rs. 2,641 Cr FY26 revenue, entirely from PSU counterparties with contractual commitments, and execution timelines of 1.5–2.5 years per project type. FY27 and FY28 revenue are, on this view, largely locked in.

**2. Operating leverage — manufacturing scaling.** Capacity scales from 2.5 GWh (FY26, 80% utilisation) to 10 GWh (Oct-26). Fixed overhead stays close to constant as throughput quadruples; in-house container fabrication adds an estimated 4–5% margin improvement. Manufacturing revenue carries 12–14% margins versus EPC's 7–10%, so the mix shift is expected to support margin expansion from FY28.

**3. Execution proof — a strong Q4 FY26.** Q4 FY26 revenue was Rs. 1,097 Cr (+60.5% YoY), the strongest quarter to date; EBITDA was Rs. 163 Cr versus Rs. 76 Cr in Q4 FY25. Management has guided to a more even quarterly distribution in FY27, implying a structurally stronger H1 FY27 relative to H1 FY26.

**4. BOO annuity model — a less-visible asset.** An Rs. 4,433 Cr BOO order book at a 12–13% project-level IRR. The MSEDCL project alone is guided to contribute Rs. 800–1,000 Cr of FY27 revenue under a lease model paying Rs. 2.19 lakh/MW/month; three of four BOO projects are funded internally or via IPO proceeds. Finance-lease income of Rs. 558 Cr was recognised in FY26, with a management target of positive operating cash flow by FY28.

**5. Small-cap re-rating potential.** A recently listed company (FY26 IPO) with limited institutional coverage. As BESS manufacturing revenue comes to dominate the FY27 P&L, the analyst's expectation is a market re-classification from "telecom EPC" toward "energy manufacturer" — a category where peers trade at 20–35x P/E, versus the roughly 12.5x the stock currently trades at.

## 8. Management Assessment

**Credibility and track record.** Chairman & MD Venugopal Rao Maddisetty has navigated three business pivots — power, then telecom, then BESS — each timed ahead of a structural demand inflection, per company disclosures. The decision to move directly to 10 GWh rather than pausing at 5 GWh is framed by management as demand-led rather than speculative.

**Capital allocation.** The Rs. 819 Cr in IPO proceeds has been deployed toward BOO project capex and manufacturing expansion; three of four BOO projects are funded through the IPO or internal accruals, with management stating no new BOO commitments will be made without external funding secured first. The credit rating upgrade from BBB- to A- within twelve months is an independent, third-party data point on balance-sheet quality.

**Concall consistency.** Guidance has been maintained across quarters: FY27E revenue of Rs. 3,200–3,400 Cr, FY28E of Rs. 4,000–4,200 Cr, and PAT margins of 10–11% for FY27. A commissioning delay of roughly two months (not a full quarter) was attributed to a West Asia shipping disruption, which management and this note both characterise as industry-wide rather than execution-specific. No auditor qualifications appear in the RHP's restated financials.

**Key watch item.** The promoter family constitutes the executive management team; independent directors with backgrounds at CSIR, SBI and SEBI sit on the board. FY27 delivery against guidance will be the company's first major test as a listed entity.

## 10. Valuation — Multi-Methodology Approach

**A. DCF (base case).** WACC of 13.5% (risk-free rate 7% + beta 1.2 × equity risk premium 5.5%, rounded), terminal growth of 5.0%, forecast period FY27E–FY32E, terminal EV/EBITDA of 12x. Implied value per share: Rs. 220–235.

**B. Forward P/E.** FY27E EPS of Rs. 17.7 at a target multiple of 13x implies Rs. 230 — a 35–40% discount to the energy-manufacturer peer average of roughly 20–22x. FY28E EPS of Rs. 22.9 at 12x implies Rs. 275.

**C. EV/EBITDA.** FY27E EBITDA of Rs. 561 Cr at a target 10x multiple implies an enterprise value of Rs. 5,610 Cr; less an estimated Rs. 400 Cr FY27E net debt implies roughly Rs. 256 per share. 10x sits below the peer range of 12–18x.

**D. Valuation reconciliation.** Blending DCF (Rs. 228, 40% weight), forward P/E (Rs. 230, 40% weight) and EV/EBITDA (Rs. 256, 20% weight) produces a blended target price of Rs. 230.

> **Analyst takeaway:** Three independent methods converge around Rs. 230 — a 35–40% discount to manufacturing-sector peers that, in this note's assessment, is a function of small-cap and early-listed status rather than business fundamentals.

## 12. Scenario Analysis

**Bull case — Rs. 336 (+78%).** FY27 revenue of Rs. 3,600 Cr, FY26–28 CAGR of roughly 28%, EBITDA margin of 18–19%, FY27E EPS above Rs. 21, target multiple of 19x FY27E P/E. Triggers: 10 GWh at 85%+ utilisation, cell manufacturing announced, NEC Africa orders materialise in Q1 FY27, C&I revenue beats expectations, and re-rating to peer multiples occurs faster than expected.

**Base case (official) — Rs. 230 (+22%).** FY27 revenue of Rs. 3,300 Cr, FY26–28 CAGR of roughly 25%, EBITDA margin of 17.0%, FY27E EPS of Rs. 17.7, target multiple of 13x FY27E P/E. Assumes management guidance is achieved, historical execution continues, a peer-consistent discount multiple persists, and working capital normalises by September 2026 as guided.

**Bear case — Rs. 130 (-31%).** FY27 revenue of Rs. 2,700 Cr, FY26–28 CAGR of roughly 12%, EBITDA margin of 15–16%, FY27E EPS of Rs. 13, target multiple of 10x FY27E P/E. Triggers: a significant cell-price surge erodes margins, the 10 GWh commissioning is delayed further, PSU order cancellations occur, working-capital strain worsens, and the multiple compresses to 10x.

## 13. Earnings Bridge — CMP Rs. 189 to Target Rs. 230

The path from the current order book to the target price runs through several linked steps: (1) an Rs. 11,338 Cr order book (4.3x FY26 revenue), all PSU counterparties, 78.1% energy-driven; (2) order execution beginning in H1 FY27 against Rs. 3,200–3,400 Cr management guidance, with a more even quarterly distribution than FY26's Q4-heavy pattern; (3) revenue growth of roughly 25% YoY to Rs. 3,300 Cr in FY27E, as the energy share of revenue rises from roughly 45% toward 65%; (4) operating leverage from 10 GWh capacity and container fabrication, with finance costs falling to an estimated Rs. 75 Cr from Rs. 115 Cr; (5) EBITDA growth of roughly 23% YoY to Rs. 561 Cr, driven mainly by volume rather than margin expansion in FY27; (6) PAT growth of roughly 17% YoY to Rs. 360 Cr, aided by finance-cost relief; (7) EPS growth of roughly 17.2% to Rs. 17.7 on a stable share count; and (8) a modest multiple re-rating from roughly 12.5x to a 13x FY27E P/E target as the market gradually reclassifies the business from telecom EPC toward energy infrastructure manufacturing.

## 14. Conclusion

India's first vertically-integrated BESS platform — spanning cell-to-pack-to-container manufacturing, integrated PCS/EMS, grid-scale EPC and BOO asset ownership — is, in this note's assessment, priced on telecom-EPC multiples while its manufacturing capacity, order book composition and third-party validation (including the L&T order) already resemble those of an energy-infrastructure manufacturer. The Rs. 230 target reflects a 13x FY27E P/E, a 35–40% discount to energy-manufacturing peers, supported by a DCF cross-check of Rs. 220–235 and an EV/EBITDA cross-check of roughly Rs. 256. The risk/reward set out in Section 12 — Rs. 336 bull case against Rs. 130 bear case — is, in the analyst's view, asymmetric to the upside from the current price, though this depends on execution of the FY27 capacity ramp and order-book conversion discussed in Sections 5 and 11.
